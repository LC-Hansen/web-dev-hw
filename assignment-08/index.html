Step 1 : Ingredients

© I do not own this image. Sourced from Target.

The ingredients for this dish are just so easy, its one that has become a staple for me and my family.

Servings 2 Prep 5 minutes Total cook time, 15 min
  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps without the stems

  • A pizza sauce of your liking, you could even sub out the sauce if that is to your liking

  • Pizza toppings, my favorites are black olives, italian sausage, with cheese and a little red pepper to really jazz it up

Step 2 : Prep

© I do not own this image. Sourced from Cafe Delites

This part is really up to you as well. You have two options as I see it, bake at 350 ferenheit for 10-12 minutes, or broil at 500 and keep an eye on them.
Either option works well.

Make sure to clean the mushrooms well and remove any leftover stems

Optionally you can bake the mushroom caps for 3-5 minutes and let them realease some of their water before baking on higher heat with the ingredients inside.


The only neccessity to prep is that you cook any raw meats now as they will not have time to cook in the oven and would not be safe to eat.

Step 3 : Put it all together

© I do not own this image. Sourced from The Mountain Kitchen.

This recipe is so simple we made it this far in about 5 minutes

Now you just add the ingredients to your liking, I prefer mine to be saucy and eaten with a fork, others prefer something they can eat with their hands more like a true pizza replacement

Throw the now completed mushrooms into a well oiled cast iron pan, or onto an oiled baking sheet, put it in the oven and wait!

It seriously is that easy!
